    Flickr Header...

    18/07/2011

    So I've had a bit of a thing for using API's recently and just seeing what could be done. I wired up a jQuery function to ajax in json from a flickr search and thought 'This is pretty sweet!...how can I use it though!?'. I sat down and started designing this site (in the browser as you can probably tell), and thought, 'man, I really can't be arsed to open Photoshop and start manipulating imag... hang on!' - and thus the header was born.

    Performance

    Yes. the header does make 4 AJAX requests to retrieve all of the images shown. Yes. this is 4 whole separate requests to be made straight away on page load. Yes. this will never be cached as the images are loaded externally each time. Yes. if you have a shitty connection, chances are you're not going to like it.

    My reasoning

    But. this is taking design in a browser to the next level, I didn't have to open Photoshop once to achieve a fully designed header. And. this is an incredibly easy way to show everything that's relevant to me, and to who Iam as a person in one area. And. everytime someone uploads a new photo to flickr with one of these keywords, hey guess what? My header changes. In theory, this thing could completely change every 5 seconds, no randomize functions, no database full of items to pull out, no massive overhead from the code that I've had to write. And. if you're a normal person and an avid web user, chances are you have a decent connection and this thing is going to take you a couple of seconds to download. And. from a webdev point of view, how frickin' cool is that thing!?

    Every now and again we have to step out of that extremely compact and strict professional network that we're used to to try something new, and learn something new. Personal sites and blogs are the perfect place to do this, hey, I'm not saying start ajaxing half of the internet into your website because you can't be arsed to write your own content or make your own design, but is this really that bad every now and again? Personally I think it's pretty damn awesome.

    Tags: jQuery, ajax, json, flickr
